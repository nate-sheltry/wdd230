Design Principles

Repitition

D&D Beyond https://www.dndbeyond.com/

This use to be the design for all people who visited the site, it recently has changed to be for only logged in users, however the 4 main items to interact with present in the screenshot are first shown at the top of the page, a feed, then once against at the bottom. The feed itself uses a square grid with the bottom half of the squares being the title and the top half being a thumbnail.

Contrast

Plex https://www.plex.tv/

This site uses contrast well as the white text against gradient background helps it to stand out, followed by the orange button against the blue-ish hue behind it, which is then proceeded by the various shows and movies thumbnails skewed in the background right below it, creating alot of contrast between the background's top and bottom half as well as the text and button above it.

Proximity

Steam https://store.steampowered.com/

The Steam store webpage packs alot together in a small place and keeps like minded things together very well while still being very presentable. This is evident by their header present in the lower part of the screenshot, which is one of many on their page used to organize its content into isolated places allowing users to scroll and select what they want quickly and effectively.
They also keep their various webpage categories towards the top as well as a button for more options in the top left leading to a very compact and effective presentation.
